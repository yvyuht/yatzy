Rule Information
In each turn, a player can roll the dice up to three times. However, during the second and third rolls, the player doesn't need to roll all five dice again. Instead, they can choose to set aside some dice that show the numbers they want to keep. For instance, if a player rolls 1, 3, 3, 4, and 6 and aims for a sequence like 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 (known as a large straight), they can keep the 1, 3, and 4, and roll only the remaining 3 and 6, hoping to get 2 and 5..
